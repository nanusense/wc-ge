01

What This Project Is

Malhar has been lived in for fourteen years. Seven communities are occupied. Three are under construction. Over that time, residents have built something that goes well beyond the physical infrastructure: a culture of composting, wildlife coexistence, art, enterprise, and shared responsibility.

The Archival Project documents that culture. It rethinks malhar.community as the platform that tells this story, supported by a redesigned directory and a WhatsApp chatbot that meets residents where they already communicate.

The project delivers three things:

  • A revamped malhar.community, structured around the narrative layers that define life at Malhar, not around individual communities. writeclick defines the content architecture and editorial direction; design and development are executed by external or internal resources under writeclick's coordination.
  • A redesigned directory with a WhatsApp chatbot, so residents can find services and information without leaving the app they already use. writeclick defines the requirements and content structure; technical build is resourced separately.
  • A path to community editorial, where a small team of resident editors handles day-to-day content under writeclick's ongoing mentorship.
02

Two Problems to Solve First

The Trust Problem

Some residents see malhar.community as a GoodEarth marketing initiative. That perception, whether fully accurate or not, is a real barrier to adoption and contribution.

The Adoption Problem

The website has a directory. Nobody uses it. Residents rely on an Excel file pinned in a WhatsApp group. The directory's content isn't the problem. Its location is.

Addressing Trust

  • GoodEarth's branding recedes. The site becomes "Malhar Community." GoodEarth is acknowledged as builder and facilitator, not positioned as editorial voice or protagonist.
  • All content is resident-voiced. writeclick documents and edits, but the voice belongs to the people who live here.
  • writeclick holds editorial independence. GoodEarth reviews for accuracy and sensitivity, not brand alignment.
  • Contributors are credited by name. The platform visibly belongs to the people who contribute to it.
  • A community editorial team is established, ideally young adults from Malhar, who handle day-to-day content under writeclick's continued mentorship. When residents are the visible face of the platform's content, the trust question is addressed structurally.

Addressing Adoption

  • The WhatsApp chatbot is the primary interface. Residents message the bot with a query and get an immediate response. Zero behaviour change required.
  • The website directory is the backend, not the front door. Same data feeds both. The bot is for speed. The website is for browsing.
  • The directory redesign is driven by actual usage patterns. writeclick audits WhatsApp groups to understand what residents actually ask for and how they phrase it. Categories and priority are shaped by real queries.
03

The Narrative Layers

The core of the revamped site is a set of narrative layers. Each documents one broad dimension of life at Malhar. Each holds multiple sub-layers that grow over time.

The individual communities (Footprints, Mosaic, Resonance, Terraces, Medley) appear naturally within the layers, not as separate features. They are the geography of the stories, not the stories themselves.

Flora

Trees, gardens, landscape

The plant life of Malhar. Trees planted and trees that arrived on their own. Gardens, green corridors, flowering seasons. How the landscape has matured over fourteen years. Sub-layers: tree inventory, garden stories, seasonal records, native vs. planted species.

Fauna

Birds, snakes, dogs, insects

The animal life that shares Malhar with its residents. The programmes residents have built around coexistence and welfare. Sub-layers: bird inventory, snake rescue log, stray dog welfare, insect documentation, coexistence protocols.

The Commons

Systems, protocols, governance

What residents have built and maintained together. The collective infrastructure. Sub-layers: wet waste composting, water management, sustainability initiatives, community governance, shared protocols.

The Culture

Art, performance, celebration

The creative and social life that residents have invented for themselves. Sub-layers: Art at Adda, Courtyard Koota, Spirit of Malhar, book clubs, comedy, music, festivals.

Made at Malhar

Businesses, ventures, craft

Businesses, creative ventures, professional practices, and passion projects built from within Malhar. Sub-layers: individual maker profiles, origin stories, home-grown brands, resident services.

Directory and WhatsApp Chatbot

Directory Redesign

writeclick defines the content structure, categories, and priorities. UX design and technical implementation are handled by design and development resources, coordinated by writeclick. The redesign focuses on three priorities:

  • Findability. Fewer categories. Clearer labels. A search bar that works. Most-used categories, identified from actual WhatsApp group queries, surfaced first.
  • Maintainability. Simple enough for the community editorial team to add, update, or remove listings without developer involvement.
  • Bot compatibility. Structured data that feeds both the website and the WhatsApp chatbot from a single source.

    • WhatsApp Chatbot

    The chatbot is the primary access layer. It meets residents where they already are. writeclick defines the requirements, content mapping, and query logic. Development and deployment are handled by a technical resource, either sourced by writeclick or provided by GoodEarth.

    06

    Community Editorial Team

    The long-term success of malhar.community depends on regular, resident-driven content. The project builds toward this by establishing a small community editorial team during the engagement.

    During the ten-month engagement, writeclick holds full editorial ownership. In the final months, writeclick identifies, recruits, and mentors a small team of resident editors, ideally young adults from Malhar who are digitally fluent and interested in storytelling or community work.

    After the engagement, writeclick continues as editorial mentor and oversight. The community editors handle day-to-day content. writeclick provides guidance, quality checks, and periodic reviews. The relationship shifts from writeclick producing everything to writeclick overseeing while the editorial team manages ongoing content.

    What the editorial team handles

    • Publishing new content to the narrative layers.
    • Maintaining the directory and responding to listing requests.
    • Monitoring the WhatsApp chatbot and updating its data.
    • Managing the Voices section.
    • Updating the Events section.

    What they do not handle

    Site architecture, editorial standards, strategic direction, and coordination of design and development resources. writeclick retains that role.

    Why this matters: When residents are the visible face of the platform's content, and editorial oversight comes from an independent party rather than the builder, the trust question is addressed structurally. It also creates a meaningful role for young people in the community.

    What GoodEarth Needs to Provide

    • Single point of contact. One person to coordinate across all deliverables.
    • Access to existing content. goodearth.org.in admin, malhar.community (Webflow), and the GoodEarth YouTube channel.
    • Access to WhatsApp community groups. For the query audit. writeclick observes query patterns, not private conversations.
    • Institutional knowledge. Founding stories, design decisions, early history for each narrative layer.
    • Resident introductions. Two to three per narrative layer.
    • Directory data validation. Review audit for accuracy. Flag outdated listings.
    • Design and development resources. GoodEarth provides or jointly sources the design/UX and development resources needed for the site revamp, directory redesign, and chatbot build. writeclick coordinates these resources but does not execute UX design or technical development.
    • Approval turnaround. Five working days. Reviews are for accuracy and sensitivity only.
    • Web team availability. Webflow support during Months 1 to 3. Publishing support Months 4 to 10.
    • Support for editorial team recruitment. Help identify young adults. Designate internal liaison for ongoing coordination.
